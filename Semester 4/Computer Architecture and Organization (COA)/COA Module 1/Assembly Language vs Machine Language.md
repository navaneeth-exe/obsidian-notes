## **Definition**

- **Machine Language**: Lowest-level language consisting of **binary (0s and 1s)** executed directly by the CPU.
- **Assembly Language**: Low-level language that uses **mnemonics (like ADD, MOV)** to represent machine instructions.

---

## **Comparison Table**

|Feature|Machine Language|Assembly Language|
|---|---|---|
|Representation|Binary (0s and 1s)|Mnemonics (ADD, SUB)|
|Readability|Very difficult|Easier than machine language|
|Execution|Directly executed by CPU|Needs assembler|
|Programming|Error-prone|Easier to write|
|Speed|Fastest|Slightly slower (due to translation)|
